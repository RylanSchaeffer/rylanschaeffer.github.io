History of Computer Science

I taught a 1-unit seminar Spring 2015 on the History of Computer Science. In case you're interested in learning more about it, here is the updated syllabus. Out of the 15 students in the class, 11 submitted course evaluations and 9 submitted the modified course evaluation. You can read their responses soon, when I figure out how to format the data in a way that doesn't hurt my eyes. I want to publicly thank Sean Davis for serving as my instructor of record, as well as Nina Amenta, Phillip Rogaway, Matthew Farrens, Norm Matloff, Vladimir Filkov, Patrice Koehl and Lori Avellar for making the seminar possible.

I intend to teach one course Spring 2016, either on homomorphic encryption, privacy securing communication technologies (e.g. TOR) or cryptocurrencies (e.g. Bitcoin).
